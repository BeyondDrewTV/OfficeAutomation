# Brand Voice
# Product: After-Hours Lead Capture (Missed Call Text-Back)

## Voice Summary
Direct, human, practical. One person talking to another.
Not an agency. Not a software company. Not a consultant.

## Tone Attributes

1. **Short** — emails under 100 words. Proposals under one page.
2. **Specific** — "plumber" not "service business." "10pm call" not "after-hours inquiry."
3. **Outcome-first** — lead captured, owner notified, job not lost. Not "system deployed."
4. **Honest** — we capture leads. We do not guarantee jobs or revenue.
5. **Calm** — no urgency theater, no scarcity tactics, no hype.

## Style Rules
- Write like you're texting a business owner, not pitching a boardroom
- Avoid all agency-speak: "leverage," "streamline," "optimize," "solution," "ecosystem"
- Avoid all AI-speak: "AI-powered," "intelligent automation," "machine learning"
- Use "we set up" and "it sends a text" — not "the system triggers a workflow"
- If a sentence has more than 20 words, split it

## Phrases We Use
- "When someone calls after hours and no one answers, they usually call the next company."
- "We set up a text-back line so that lead doesn't disappear."
- "They get a text. You get a notification. Done."
- "We handle the setup. You don't touch anything technical."

## Phrases We Never Use
- "AI automation" / "workflow automation" / "automation solution"
- "Guaranteed results" / "guaranteed revenue"
- "Leverage your existing stack"
- "Seamless integration"
- "Game-changer" / "cutting-edge" / "robust" / "scalable"
- "Revolutionize" / "optimize" / "streamline"
